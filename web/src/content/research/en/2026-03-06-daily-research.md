**The U.S. Department of Defense designated an AI company as a "supply chain risk" — and that same week, the company discovered 22 security vulnerabilities in the world's most-used browser. Anthropic's week was peak irony.** 🟢 Observed

Defense Secretary Pete Hegseth officially designated Anthropic as a supply chain risk, prompting defense contractors to preemptively stop using Claude "just in case." CEO Dario Amodei wrote a 1,600-word internal memo: "We did not donate to Trump, nor did we send dictator-style praise." The context is clear — while OpenAI lined up at the White House, Anthropic chose political neutrality and is paying the price. 🟢 Observed

But here's the twist. After the blacklisting, Claude's daily new signups hit all-time highs. According to AppFigures data, the app ranks #1 in free and AI categories across U.S., Canada, and European app stores. The government punishes; the market embraces. 🟢 Observed

The same week, Anthropic published results from its Mozilla collaboration. Claude Opus 4.6 discovered 22 security vulnerabilities in Firefox, 14 classified as high-severity — nearly one-fifth of all high-severity Firefox vulnerabilities found in 2025. Notably, a Use After Free vulnerability was found just 20 minutes into JavaScript engine exploration, compressing work that takes human security researchers days to weeks. The patches were deployed to hundreds of millions of users in Firefox 148.0. 🟢 Observed

Meanwhile, OpenAI entered the security space in earnest. They launched Codex Security, a new research preview agent specialized in app security vulnerability detection and remediation. Through the Codex Open Source Fund, open-source developers get six months of ChatGPT Pro subscriptions plus Codex Security access. More noteworthy is the report that OpenAI is developing a GitHub competitor. Sparked by recent GitHub outages, the project is still months away, but if completed, it creates the curious dynamic of OpenAI directly competing with its biggest investor, Microsoft. 🔵 Supported

In the EU, Meta announced it would allow rival AI chatbots on WhatsApp. A move to appease EU antitrust regulators, it will support general-purpose AI chatbots through WhatsApp Business API on a paid basis for 12 months. Compared to the previous blocking of ChatGPT, Copilot, and others on WhatsApp, it's a meaningful shift — though the "paid" caveat raises questions about how real the openness will be. 🟡 Speculative

**So what?** The power map of the AI industry is being redrawn rapidly. In a world where technical capability and political power have diverged, Anthropic proves itself through technology while being punished through politics. OpenAI is swallowing coding infrastructure, and Meta is opening its gates under regulatory pressure. All of this happened in a single week.
