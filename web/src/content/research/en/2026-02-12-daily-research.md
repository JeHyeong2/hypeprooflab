**Yesterday offered a masterclass in irony. [OpenAI dissolved its Mission Alignment team](https://techcrunch.com/2026/02/11/openai-disbands-mission-alignment-team-which-focused-on-safe-and-trustworthy-ai-development/) — the group dedicated to keeping AI safe and trustworthy — while [SpaceX unveiled plans to build AI data centers in orbit](https://techcrunch.com/2026/02/11/why-the-economics-of-orbital-ai-are-so-brutal/). One company abandoning safety guardrails, another literally launching problems into space.**

## Money Does the Talking

The investment market is spelling out its AI conviction in hard numbers. [Runway raised $315 million at a $5.3 billion valuation](https://techcrunch.com/2026/02/10/ai-video-startup-runway-raises-315m-at-5-3b-valuation-eyes-more-capable-world-models/), and the [former GitHub CEO's startup secured a record $60 million seed round](https://techcrunch.com/2026/02/10/former-github-ceo-raises-record-60m-dev-tool-seed-round-at-300m-valuation/) for developer tools.

Runway's "world model" approach is particularly intriguing. Rather than simply generating video, the company aims to build AI that understands the laws of physics — a bet that today's generative AI will eventually evolve into genuine intelligence. SpaceX's orbital data center plan takes this ambition to another dimension entirely: 100 GW of computing power distributed across a million satellites, fueled by unlimited solar energy. TechCrunch admitted the economics are "brutally difficult," but if it works, the paradigm for AI training changes forever.

## Reality Check: Security as an Afterthought

Back on Earth, the picture is far less glamorous. [OpenAI's dissolution of its Mission Alignment team](https://techcrunch.com/2026/02/11/openai-disbands-mission-alignment-team-which-focused-on-safe-and-trustworthy-ai-development/) signals more than a reorg — it's a philosophical shift. Getting to market now matters more than ensuring AI systems align with human values.

The OpenClaw security debacle underscores the point. [SecurityScorecard's investigation](https://www.infosecurity-magazine.com/news/researchers-40000-exposed-openclaw/) found that of 135,000 exposed instances, over 50,000 are vulnerable to remote code execution. As AI agents go mainstream, security is actually regressing.

Microsoft isn't exempt either. Its [February Patch Tuesday addressed 54 vulnerabilities](https://blog.qualys.com/vulnerabilities-threat-research/2026/02/10/microsoft-patch-tuesday-february-2026-security-update-review/), including a command injection flaw affecting GitHub Copilot. When even AI coding tools can be hacked, nobody's safe.

## New Weapons in the Developer Arsenal

The developer ecosystem is evolving rapidly despite the chaos. [AWS and Google Cloud joining forces on multicloud networking](https://www.infoq.com/news/2025/12/aws-gcp-multicloud-networking/) was an unexpected alliance — likely driven by market pressure to ease vendor lock-in. [Google's LangExtract hit GitHub's #1 trending spot](https://trendshift.io/), offering LLM-powered extraction of structured data from unstructured text — a preview of how AI-era data pipelines will work.

Meanwhile, [n8n added LLM chat capabilities](https://releasebot.io/updates/n8n) to its workflow automation platform. The age of automating work through AI conversations — no code required — has arrived.

## AI Reinvents the User Experience

Social media is experimenting with AI in new ways. [Threads' "Dear Algo" feature](https://techcrunch.com/2026/02/11/threads-new-dear-algo-ai-feature-lets-you-personalize-your-feed/) lets users directly instruct the algorithm — "show me this, hide that." It's a clever approach that simultaneously increases algorithmic transparency and deepens personalization. [Facebook added AI-powered animated profile photos and text post backgrounds](https://techcrunch.com/2026/02/10/facebook-adds-new-ai-features-animated-profile-photos-and-backgrounds-for-text-posts/), which look fun on the surface but are transparently designed to harvest more user data and extend platform dwell time. [Amazon's exploration of an AI training data marketplace](https://techcrunch.com/2026/02/10/amazon-may-launch-a-marketplace-where-media-sites-can-sell-their-content-to-ai-companies/) is intriguing too — a new revenue stream for content creators, a legal data source for AI companies, and naturally, a commission opportunity for Amazon.

## What to Watch

Today's news paints a clear pattern: the AI industry is choosing speed over safety. OpenAI's safety team dissolution, OpenClaw's security crisis, and plans to colonize orbit with data centers all point in the same direction. The real question is when this "speed-first" strategy hits a wall — whether through regulatory intervention, a major security breach, or investor confidence collapse.

One thing is clear: the future of AI isn't being decided by technology alone. It's a game of money and power. And in that game, users and developers remain spectators.
