**The second week of February 2026 brought an unexpected shake-up to the AI power rankings. With the launch of Claude Opus 4.6, Anthropic surged to [68% odds of leading the AI model market](https://markets.financialcontent.com/stocks/article/predictstreet-2026-2-9-anthropics-coup-claude-46-dominates-ai-prediction-markets-with-68-odds) — a figure that skyrocketed from 40% in mere hours. Meanwhile, OpenAI cratered to 6%, its lowest point since GPT-4's debut.**

## The Agent Wars: Fifteen Minutes That Changed Everything

In a scene straight out of a corporate thriller, OpenAI and Anthropic had both [scheduled agent coding tool launches for the same time slot — 10 AM PST](https://techcrunch.com/2026/02/05/openai-launches-new-agentic-coding-model-only-minutes-after-anthropic-drops-its-own/). Anthropic struck first, beating its rival by 15 minutes. The precision of this timing game reveals just how fierce the competition between the two has become.

Apple jumped into the fray as well. The company [announced agentic coding support in Xcode 26.3](https://www.apple.com/newsroom/2026/02/xcode-26-point-3-unlocks-the-power-of-agentic-coding/), offering deep integration with both Anthropic's Claude Agent and OpenAI's Codex. Developers can now experience an entirely new paradigm inside Xcode — AI agents decomposing tasks, making architecture-informed decisions, and leveraging built-in tools autonomously.

## The Great Capital Migration: $1.3 Trillion Headed for AI Infrastructure

The money tells its own story. A staggering [$1.3 trillion is earmarked for AI infrastructure by 2027](https://www.cnbc.com/2026/02/09/this-ai-play-just-hit-another-record-high-what-were-making-of-the-stocks-gains.html), with $660 billion concentrated in this year alone. Alphabet announced it would double its capital expenditure to $175–185 billion for AI computing capacity, and Meta plans to invest up to $185 billion.

The stock market responded accordingly. [Corning shares surged 7% after a Wall Street Journal "AI Superstar" feature](https://www.fool.com/investing/2026/02/09/artificial-intelligence-ai-is-changing-corporate-s/), bringing its year-to-date gains to nearly 50%. Microsoft, however, received a downgrade from Melius Research — analysts noted that CEO Satya Nadella had "lost the AI narrative" and that the Copilot-centric strategy was underperforming expectations.

## Ads and Ethics: OpenAI's New Gamble

OpenAI [announced on February 10 that it would begin testing ads inside ChatGPT](https://www.thenews.com.pk/latest/1391748-anthropic-criticises-chatgpt-ads-as-openai-begins-testing-advertising-in-ai-chats). Initially limited to a subset of free and Go-tier users in the US, the move drew an immediate sharp rebuke from Anthropic. The philosophical divide over AI monetization is shaping up to be a new fault line between the two rivals.

## Supply Chain Attacks Evolve: A Real-World Warning

On the cybersecurity front, alarm bells are ringing. [Rapid7 reported a supply chain attack that planted a backdoor through a compromised Notepad++ distribution](https://thehackernews.com/2026/02/weekly-recap-ai-skill-malware-31tbps.html). The Lotus Blossom APT group exploited the implicit trust users place in routine software downloads — a particularly concerning vector at a time when AI tool distribution is exploding.

Simultaneously, [Singapore's government confirmed that several major telecom operators were targeted by the China-linked UNC3886 group](https://thehackernews.com/2026/02/weekly-recap-ai-skill-malware-31tbps.html). The attacks were intercepted before causing service disruptions, but the incident underscores how nation-state cyber operations are growing more sophisticated alongside AI infrastructure expansion.

The AI industry has entered a complex phase where technological breakthroughs, geopolitical tensions, and new business model experiments are all unfolding simultaneously. Anthropic's rapid ascent proves the market remains deeply fluid, and the rest of 2026 promises even more unpredictable shifts ahead.
