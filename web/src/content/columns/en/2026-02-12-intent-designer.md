In 2026, the landscape of research labs worldwide has fundamentally transformed. Where AI was once a mere "assistant" that drafted papers or summarized data, today's AI autonomously formulates experimental hypotheses, writes complex simulation code, and even debates with fellow agents. At the center of this shift stand **OpenClaw**, symbolizing the explosive energy of open source, and **Claude Code**, the pinnacle of enterprise-grade tooling.

## The Evolution of Tools: Thinking Runtimes and Programmatic Tool Calling

OpenClaw (formerly Clawdbot/Moltbot) went viral immediately after release, amassing over 20,000 GitHub stars within 24 hours. Running in the user's local environment, it empowered people to control systems through messaging apps like WhatsApp and Telegram — as naturally as texting a friend. Meanwhile, Anthropic's Claude Code introduced **Programmatic Tool Calling (PTC)**, a technical innovation that reduced token consumption by 37% while autonomously managing complex codebases with surgical precision.

These advances are compressing the speed of research exponentially:

| Research Field | Key Changes & Achievements | Related Tech/Cases |
| :---- | :---- | :---- |
| **Physics** | 6-month simulation code rewritten in 2 weeks | JAX-based simulation acceleration |
| **Aerospace** | 100% automated UAV wing design optimization | OpenAeroStruct, Foam-Agent |
| **Life Sciences** | 10x faster genomic analysis and literature review | Claude for Life Sciences |
| **Theoretical Research** | Automation from hypothesis to paper to peer review | Sakana AI 'The AI Scientist' |

## Accelerating Discovery: From Simulations to Autonomous Labs

Physics researchers now delegate the modeling and debugging of complex theories — string theory, loop quantum gravity — at 11-dimensional scales to agents. In one case, researchers built a simulator implementing Earnshaw's Theorem, then deployed a separate verification agent to validate physical plausibility with high machine precision.

Aerospace is equally revolutionary. Hierarchical multi-agent architectures adopted by NASA and others orchestrate aerodynamics, structural, and acoustic specialist agents, compressing design pipelines that once took weeks into mere hours. In life sciences, companies like Schrödinger and 10x Genomics are already using Claude Code to lower barriers in drug modeling and single-cell analysis.

## The Leaders' Vision: "A Country of Geniuses in a Datacenter"

Industry leaders agree this is only the beginning. Anthropic's Dario Amodei has suggested AGI may arrive around 2027, describing it as **"a country of geniuses in a datacenter"** — millions of genius-level intelligences connected in parallel, deployed against cancer and energy challenges.

Sam Altman has likewise heralded the **"Intelligence Age,"** where AI agents function as personal expert research teams. Demis Hassabis envisions AI evolving into **"World Models"** that understand and simulate the laws of the physical world, ultimately solving every global disease.

## Shadows and Challenges: Security Risks and Ethical Responsibility

Behind this brilliant future lie deep shadows. OpenClaw's autonomy exposed serious security vulnerabilities — over 40,000 instances were found exposed on the internet, with 63% vulnerable to remote code execution (RCE) attacks. On Moltbook, the social network where agents communicate autonomously, 1.5 million API tokens were leaked.

Institutional responses are accelerating. *Nature* and *Science* refuse to list AI agents as co-authors and now require detailed disclosure of AI usage in research. On intellectual property, the trend is clear: "pure AI-generated" works with minimal human contribution are denied copyright protection.

## Conclusion: The Researcher's New Core Competency — "Agency"

The researcher's role is evolving from lab worker to **"strategist"** and **"designer of intent"** — someone who orchestrates multiple agents to create value. In an era where the speed of discovery has multiplied tenfold, a researcher's competitive edge is no longer "how much you know" but the **"agency"** to direct complex AI systems while making ethical and strategic judgments.

We stand at the threshold of the Intelligence Age. Inside the door that OpenClaw and Claude Code have flung open, the synergy of "co-science" between humans and AI is already tackling humanity's most enduring challenges. The extraordinary journey has begun.
