# The Day a Lawyer Beat Every Developer

Anthropic released the winner list of their Claude Code hackathon. Gold went to a California personal injury lawyer. Bronze to a Belgian head of cardiology. Other winners included a musician and an infrastructure engineer. Software developers were virtually absent. At an AI coding tool hackathon.

This wasn't a fluke. The results quietly point to where the center of gravity of competitiveness is shifting in the AI era.

---

## When Building Gets Easy, What Gets Hard?

The gold-winning lawyer had witnessed the pain of denied building permits countless times in his legal practice. The cardiologist had watched patients leave the hospital every day without understanding their diagnosis. What they built weren't flashy tech demos. They were answers to pain they'd personally witnessed.

AI is rapidly lowering the barrier to implementation. Even without knowing code, if you can explain *what* to build to tools like Claude Code, you're halfway there. As the scarcity of implementation skills declines, something else is becoming truly scarce: **knowing which problem to solve.** And the people who know that are in the field.

Here's a question for you: what field are you in?

For the lawyer, it was legal practice. For the doctor, the examination room. For the father, time spent with his daughter. Your field might be your workplace, your daily routine, or a hobby you've been devoted to for years. The experience, intuition, and discomforts you've taken for granted in that field — these have become assets that actually work in the AI era.

Thoughts that used to end with "I wish this existed, but I can't build it" are now seeds of products. Before learning technology, there's something more important to do: take a fresh look at what you already know.

---

## What You Can Do Right Now

No need to start big. Look back on your day and ask yourself:

> "Was there a discomfort I just tolerated and moved past today?"

That's your starting point. A Ugandan infrastructure engineer started with the under-construction roads in his own country. The narrower and more specific the problem, the fewer competitors and the greater the urgency. AI is already ready to turn that urgency into reality.

The tools are here. What's needed now is the courage to surface that discomfort you've been enduring.
