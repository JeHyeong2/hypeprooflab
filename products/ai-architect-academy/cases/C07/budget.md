# C07 — Theme Park Popup Workshop: Budget

---

## 1. Per-Session Revenue

| Item | Value | Notes |
|------|-------|-------|
| Experience fee per team | **₩50,000** | Premium park experience — comparable to character photo packages or special ride add-ons |
| Teams per session | 8-10 | Conservative 8, target 10 |
| **Gross revenue per session** | **₩400,000 - ₩500,000** | |

### Why ₩50,000/Team

The full workshop charges ₩200,000 for a 4-hour premium experience. The theme park popup is:
- 90 minutes (not 4 hours)
- Impulse purchase (not pre-planned commitment)
- Walk-in context (low willingness to pay for unplanned activity)
- Competing with ₩5,000-15,000 park experiences

₩50,000 positions us as a premium park experience (well above average but still an impulse-friendly price point). It is per-team (parent + child), not per person.

> **Why not ₩30,000?** Early analysis showed that ₩30,000/team produces negative unit economics under every revenue share scenario. At ₩30,000 × 10 teams = ₩300,000 gross, even a 70% operator share (₩210,000) barely covers variable costs, leaving nothing for fixed costs or setup recovery. See Appendix A for the full ₩30,000 analysis.

Per logistics.md Section 5.3, staff receives a 30% weekend premium and 50% holiday premium. This affects the two largest variable cost items (instructor and assistant) on ~20% of sessions.

| Item | Base Cost | Peak Rate | Peak Cost | Notes |
|------|-----------|-----------|-----------|-------|
| Instructor (1) | ₩80,000 | ×1.5 (holiday max) | **₩120,000** | Weekend = ×1.3 = ₩104,000; Holiday = ×1.5 = ₩120,000; use ×1.5 for worst-case peak |
| Assistant (1) | ₩40,000 | ×1.5 | **₩60,000** | Same premium schedule as instructor |
| Consumables | ₩15,000 | — | ₩15,000 | Unchanged |
| LTE data | ₩5,000 | — | ₩5,000 | Unchanged |
| Misc (queue mgmt, ops) | ₩20,000 | — | ₩20,000 | Unchanged |
| **Total peak variable cost/session** | ₩160,000 | | **₩220,000** | +₩60,000 vs base |

**Blended average variable cost per session:**
- 80% of sessions at ₩160,000 base + 20% at ₩220,000 peak
- Blended = (0.80 × ₩160,000) + (0.20 × ₩220,000) = ₩128,000 + ₩44,000 = **₩172,000/session**

All annual projections in Section 5 use ₩172,000 blended variable cost per session.

## 3. Revenue Share Scenarios (₩50,000/team)

### 3.1 Three Revenue Share Models

**Base assumption: 10 teams/session, ₩50,000/team = ₩500,000 gross/session, ₩172,000 blended variable cost (see Section 2.1b)**

| Model | Park Share | Our Share (Dong-A + Filamentree) | Our Revenue/Session | Contribution Margin | Margin % |
|-------|-----------|--------------------------------|--------------------|--------------------|----------|
| **A: 30/70** (Operator-favorable) | ₩150,000 | ₩350,000 | ₩350,000 | **₩178,000** | 51% |
| **B: 40/60** (Balanced) | ₩200,000 | ₩300,000 | ₩300,000 | **₩128,000** | 43% |
| **C: 50/50** (Park-favorable) | ₩250,000 | ₩250,000 | ₩250,000 | **₩78,000** | 31% |

**Recommendation:** Negotiate for Model A (30/70) given that we provide all staff, equipment, and curriculum. Fall back to Model B (40/60) maximum. Model C is acceptable only if the park provides significant benefits (e.g., free storage, marketing push, app featured placement).

### 3.2 Conservative Fill Rate (8 teams/session)

| Model | Gross/Session | Our Share | Contribution Margin |
|-------|---------------|-----------|-------------------|
| A: 30/70 | ₩400,000 | ₩280,000 | ₩120,000 |
| B: 40/60 | ₩400,000 | ₩240,000 | ₩80,000 |
| C: 50/50 | ₩400,000 | ₩200,000 | ₩40,000 |

Even at 8 teams, all three models produce positive contribution margins.

### 3.3 Alternative: Fixed Space Rental + 100% Revenue

| Model | Park Gets | We Get | Risk Profile |
|-------|-----------|--------|-------------|
| Revenue share (recommended) | % of ticket sales | Remaining % | Low risk — no upfront commitment |
| Fixed monthly rental | ₩3,000,000-5,000,000/month | 100% of session revenue | Higher risk — must fill sessions to break even |
| Hybrid | ₩1,500,000/month + 20% share | 80% of revenue - rent | Medium risk |

Revenue share is recommended for the pilot period. Transition to hybrid or fixed rental only after proving consistent demand (6+ months of data).

## 4. Break-Even Analysis

### 4.1 Monthly Break-Even (₩50,000/team, 30/70 share, ₩172K blended variable cost)

| Item | Value |
|------|-------|
| Our revenue/session (70% of ₩500,000) | ₩350,000 |

At 2 sessions/day, this requires only **3 operating days per month** — easily achievable even off-peak.

## 6. Sensitivity Analysis

### What kills the economics:

| Variable | Bad Case | Impact |
|----------|----------|--------|
| Average fill drops to 5 teams/session | Revenue -37% | Operating profit drops to ₩18M (still positive) |
| Park demands 50/50 share | Our share -29% | Operating profit drops to ₩22M (still positive) |
| Price stuck at ₩30,000/team | Revenue -40% | **Operating loss** — model requires ₩50,000 minimum |
| Sessions limited to 1/day by park | Revenue -60% | **Operating loss** unless other costs reduced proportionally |

### What makes it exceptional:

| Variable | Good Case | Impact |
|----------|-----------|--------|
| Average fill at 10 teams consistently | Revenue +25% | Operating profit ₩82M |
| ₩70,000/team (premium experience) | Revenue +40% | Operating profit ₩95M |
| Park provides staff (1 assistant) | Variable cost -25% | Operating profit ₩72M |

## Appendix A: Why ₩30,000/Team Does Not Work

Early pricing analysis evaluated ₩30,000/team as an impulse-friendly price point. The results demonstrate why this was rejected.

**Base assumption: 10 teams/session, ₩30,000/team = ₩300,000 gross/session, ₩210,000 variable cost (original 3-person staff)**

| Model | Park Share | Our Share | Our Revenue | Our Revenue - Variable Cost | Margin |
|-------|-----------|-----------|-------------|---------------------------|--------|
| 30/70 (Operator-favorable) | ₩90,000 | ₩210,000 | ₩210,000 | ₩0 | 0% |
| 40/60 (Balanced) | ₩120,000 | ₩180,000 | ₩180,000 | -₩30,000 | Negative |
| 50/50 (Park-favorable) | ₩150,000 | ₩150,000 | ₩150,000 | -₩60,000 | Negative |

**Conclusion:** At ₩30,000/team, no revenue share model covers variable costs. Even the most operator-favorable split (30/70) produces zero margin before fixed costs. The model requires a minimum of **₩50,000/team** to be viable (see Section 3 for the canonical analysis).
