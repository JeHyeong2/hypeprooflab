# C07 — Theme Park Popup Workshop (Everland / Lotte World Commissioned Operation)
## Full Business Plan

---

## Executive Summary

A 90-minute "taster" version of the Future AI Leader's Academy, operated inside education zones at Korea's top theme parks (Everland, Lotte World, KidZania). The program strips the full 4-hour curriculum to its highest-impact core — Part 3: AI-directed game creation — and packages it as a walk-in/pre-booked family experience. Chosun Ilbo's existing commissioned operation at Everland validates the model; Dong-A Ilbo enters with a differentiated AI education offering that no competitor currently provides. Revenue is generated via commission or revenue-share with the park operator (park 30-50% / Dong-A + Filamentree 50-70%). The primary strategic value is not per-session profit but massive brand exposure to 1M+ annual park visitors and a conversion funnel into the full 4-hour workshop (C01). Business development requires 6-12 months of theme park procurement cycles, with a target pilot of Summer 2027.

## 1. 90-Minute Curriculum Design

### 1.1 Design Principle: Maximum Impact in Minimum Time

The full Academy delivers impact through a 4-stage arc: Mindset Shift (Part 1) -> Collaboration (Part 2) -> Game Creation (Part 3) -> Achievement (Part 4). For the 90-minute popup, we extract only Part 3 but re-engineer it with a compressed on-ramp and a micro-showcase ending.

### 1.2 90-Minute Session Structure

| Time | Duration | Phase | Activity | Notes |
|------|----------|-------|----------|-------|
| 0:00 | 5 min | **Micro On-Ramp** | 30-second AI demo ("Watch: I tell AI to make a game, and it does it in 30 seconds"), team name, role cards (Visionary/Integrator) | Replaces 40-min Part 1 with a single "wow moment" |
| 0:05 | 5 min | **Flash Planning** | Pre-printed "Game Design Card" — 3 fields only: Game Name, Main Character, How to Win. Parent helps child fill in. | Replaces 50-min Part 2 with a structured card exercise |
| 0:10 | 10 min | **Context Training** | "Bad instruction vs. Good instruction" card flip game (3 rounds). Child experiences how specificity changes AI output. | Distilled version of Context Engineering training |
| 0:20 | 40 min | **Build: AI-Directed Game Creation** | Core hands-on: child directs AI to build their game. 10-second feedback loop. 3-step checklist: 1) Character appears 2) Character moves 3) Score works. | Identical to full workshop Part 3 core, with tighter scope |
| 1:00 | 10 min | **Polish & Save** | Bug fixes, one "bonus feature" attempt, game URL generation | "Art of Giving Up" — pick ONE improvement only |
| 1:10 | 10 min | **Micro Showcase** | Each team: 30-second live demo on main screen + applause. QR code to play at home. Sticker award for every team. | Replaces 60-min Part 4 with rapid-fire demos |
| 1:20 | 5 min | **Funnel Close** | "You just made a game in 90 minutes. Imagine what you can do in 4 hours." Hand out full workshop flyer + QR pre-registration with discount code. Photo moment with completion sticker. | Critical conversion touchpoint |
| 1:25 | 5 min | **Reset** | Staff reset stations for next session | Buffer before next group |

**Total: 90 min (85 min program + 5 min reset overlap)**

### 1.3 What's Lost and How We Compensate

| Full Workshop Element | Status in 90-min | Compensation |
|-----------------------|-------------------|--------------|
| Part 1: Mindset Shift (40 min) | Compressed to 5-min demo | The "wow moment" delivers 80% of the mindset shift; deep conceptual understanding deferred to full workshop |
| Part 2: Collaboration (50 min) | Compressed to 5-min Flash Planning | Pre-printed Game Design Card with constrained choices ensures every team has a viable plan in 5 minutes |
| Part 3: Game Creation (90 min) | Preserved at 60 min (40 build + 10 polish + 10 context training) | Tighter scope (3-step checklist vs. open-ended), more aggressive template use |
| Part 4: Achievement (60 min) | Compressed to 10-min Micro Showcase | 30-sec per team (vs. 3-5 min), sticker instead of formal award ceremony |
| Parent-child deep collaboration | Reduced | Parent role is simplified to "helper" rather than full "Integrator"; deep collaboration is the upsell hook for the full workshop |

### 1.4 Template System (Critical for 90-min Viability)

90 minutes is too short for fully open-ended creation. We provide **3 game templates** pre-loaded in the development environment:

| Template | Genre | What child customizes |
|----------|-------|----------------------|
| **Dino Jump** | Side-scroller | Character sprite, obstacle type, background, speed |
| **Space Shooter** | Top-down shooter | Enemy types, weapon effects, power-ups |
| **Maze Escape** | Puzzle | Maze layout, collectibles, time limit |

Child picks one template, then directs AI to customize it. This guarantees a working game within 40 minutes even for the youngest participants.

### 1.5 Physical Asset Specifications

Two physical assets are required for every session. These specs are the binding definition for the W45-46 curriculum finalization milestone (JY Content Lead, timeline.md).

#### 1.5a Game Design Card

| Attribute | Specification |
|-----------|--------------|
| Size | A5 (148mm × 210mm) |
| Paper | 200gsm cardstock, single-sided print, matte finish |
| Layout | 3-field constrained-choice form |
| Reusability | Single-use (collected after session, recycled) |
| Quantity | 10 per session (1 per team); monthly stock: 600 |

**Field definitions:**

| Field | Input Type | Options |
|-------|-----------|---------|
| **게임 이름 (Game Name)** | Free text (hand-written) | Blank box, ~20 characters wide |
| **주인공 (Main Character)** | Checkbox + custom | ☐ 고양이 (cat) ☐ 로봇 (robot) ☐ 공룡 (dinosaur) ☐ 사람 (person) ☐ 직접 쓰기: ___ |
| **이기는 방법 (How to Win)** | Checkbox + custom | ☐ 점수를 높여라 (get high score) ☐ 끝까지 살아남아라 (survive to the end) ☐ 목표물을 피해라 (avoid obstacles) ☐ 아이템을 모아라 (collect items) ☐ 직접 쓰기: ___ |

Design rationale: Constrained choices eliminate blank-page paralysis and ensure every team has a viable game concept within the 5-minute Flash Planning window. The free-text option preserves creative ownership.

#### 1.5b Bad/Good Instruction Card Set

| Attribute | Specification |
|-----------|--------------|
| Size | A6 (105mm × 148mm) per card |
| Paper | 300gsm cardstock, laminated both sides |
| Color-coding | ❌ BAD cards: red background, white text / ✅ GOOD cards: green background, white text |
| Reusability | **Laminated — reusable across all sessions** |
| Quantity | 5 pairs (10 cards total) per set; 5 sets per zone; replacement: 1 set/quarter |

**Minimum 5 pairs (examples to print):**

| # | ❌ BAD Instruction | ✅ GOOD Instruction |
|---|-------------------|-------------------|
| 1 | "게임 만들어줘" ("Make a game") | "공룡이 하늘을 날아다니고, 스페이스바를 누르면 뛰어오르는 게임 만들어줘" ("Make a game where a dinosaur flies and jumps when spacebar is pressed") |
| 2 | "더 재밌게 만들어줘" ("Make it more fun") | "장애물을 10초마다 하나씩 추가하고, 속도를 5%씩 높여줘" ("Add one obstacle every 10 seconds and increase speed by 5% each time") |
| 3 | "배경 바꿔줘" ("Change the background") | "배경을 우주로 바꾸고, 별이 왼쪽에서 오른쪽으로 흘러가게 해줘" ("Change background to space and make stars flow left to right") |
| 4 | "캐릭터 고쳐줘" ("Fix the character") | "캐릭터가 벽에 닿으면 빨간색으로 바뀌고 1초 후 원래 색으로 돌아오게 해줘" ("Make character turn red when hitting wall and return to original color after 1 second") |
| 5 | "점수 추가해줘" ("Add a score") | "화면 오른쪽 위에 흰색 숫자로 점수를 표시하고, 장애물을 피칠 때마다 10점씩 올라가게 해줘" ("Display score in white text top-right corner, increase by 10 each time obstacle is avoided") |

Usage in session: During the Context Training phase (0:10-0:20), instructor holds up each pair back-to-back. Child reads the BAD instruction aloud, then the GOOD instruction. Teams immediately feel the difference in specificity.

| Constraint | Impact | Mitigation |
|------------|--------|------------|
| Ambient noise from rides/crowds | Difficult to hear instructor | Semi-enclosed space with sound-dampening panels; wireless headset system for instructor; avoid outdoor zones entirely |

## 4. Revenue Share Model Analysis

### 4.1 Pricing: Why ₩50,000/Team (Not ₩30,000)

Initial analysis showed that ₩30,000/team produces negative unit economics under every revenue share scenario (see budget.md Section 3.1 for full breakdown). At ₩30,000 × 10 teams = ₩300,000 gross, even a 70% operator share (₩210,000) barely covers variable costs (₩160,000-210,000), leaving nothing for fixed costs or setup recovery.

**Canonical price: ₩50,000/team** — positioned as a premium park experience comparable to character photo packages or special ride add-ons. At ₩50,000, the model becomes viable.

### 4.2 Revenue Share Scenarios (at ₩50,000/team, 10 teams/session)

| Scenario | Park Share | Our Share | Gross/Session | Park Revenue | Our Revenue | Our Margin (after ₩160K variable) |
|----------|-----------|-----------|---------------|-------------|-------------|----------------------------------|
| A: Operator-favorable (30/70) | 30% | 70% | ₩500,000 | ₩150,000 | ₩350,000 | ₩190,000 (54%) |
| B: Balanced (40/60) | 40% | 60% | ₩500,000 | ₩200,000 | ₩300,000 | ₩140,000 (47%) |
| C: Park-favorable (50/50) | 50% | 50% | ₩500,000 | ₩250,000 | ₩250,000 | ₩90,000 (36%) |

**Recommendation: Negotiate for Scenario A (30/70)** given that we provide all staff, equipment, and curriculum. Fall back to Scenario B (40/60) maximum. Scenario C is acceptable only if the park provides significant benefits (e.g., free storage, marketing push, app featured placement).

### 4.3 Alternative: Fixed Space Rental + 100% Revenue

| Model | Park Gets | We Get | Risk Profile |
|-------|-----------|--------|-------------|
| Revenue share (recommended) | % of ticket sales | Remaining % | Low risk — no upfront commitment |
| Fixed monthly rental | ₩3,000,000-5,000,000/month | 100% of session revenue | Higher risk — must fill sessions to break even |
| Hybrid | ₩1,500,000/month + 20% share | 80% of revenue - rent | Medium risk |

Revenue share is recommended for the pilot period. Transition to hybrid or fixed rental only after proving consistent demand (6+ months of data).

### 4.4 Internal Revenue Split (Dong-A + Filamentree)

From "our share" of revenue:

| Partner | Split | Rationale |
|---------|-------|-----------|
| Dong-A Ilbo | 25% of our share | Brand name, PR support, park negotiation leverage |
| Filamentree | 75% of our share | Curriculum IP, all staff, equipment, daily operations |

## 5. Funnel Strategy: 90-min Taster to Full Workshop Conversion

### 5.1 Conversion Funnel

```
Theme Park Visitors (1M+/year per park)
  └─ See signage/hear announcement (awareness): ~10% = 100,000 families
      └─ Stop and watch demo screen (interest): ~20% = 20,000 families
          └─ Book/walk-in for 90-min session (participation): ~5% = 1,000 families/year
              └─ Complete session and receive flyer (qualified lead): ~95% = 950 families
                  └─ Scan QR / register for full workshop (intent): ~15% = 143 families
                      └─ Actually attend full workshop (conversion): ~40% = 57 families/year
```

### 5.2 Conversion Rate Assumptions

| Funnel Stage | Rate | Basis |
|--------------|------|-------|
| Session completion -> QR scan | 15% | Theme park impulse: high enthusiasm but low follow-through once they leave the park |
| QR scan -> Full workshop registration | 40% | **Pilot hypothesis** — discount code (₩30,000 off) + email nurture sequence; to be validated against actual pilot data |
| **End-to-end: session -> full workshop** | **6%** | Comparable to museum gift shop "sign up for newsletter" conversion |

**Sensitivity — Low Conversion Scenario:**

The 40% QR-to-registration rate is a pilot hypothesis. Paid event conversion benchmarks typically run 10-20%. The table below shows the impact if conversion is closer to industry baseline:

| QR→Registration Rate | Annual Full Workshop Converts (per park) | Incremental Revenue |
|----------------------|------------------------------------------|---------------------|
| 40% (pilot hypothesis) | 57 families | ₩9,690,000/year |
| **10% (conservative paid benchmark)** | **14 families** | **₩2,380,000/year** |

At 10% QR-to-registration (more representative of paid conversion benchmarks): 14 full workshop conversions/year per park = ₩2.4M incremental. This is modest but does not threaten the core business case — the primary value driver is direct session revenue (₩50,105,000/year) and brand exposure (Section 6), not funnel conversion.

### 5.3 Conversion Mechanics

| Touchpoint | Action |
|------------|--------|
| Session end | Hand out branded card with: game URL QR, full workshop QR with ₩30,000 discount, "Your child made this in 90 min — imagine 4 hours" |
| D+1 email | Automated email with child's game link + full workshop dates/locations |
| D+7 email | "Your game has been played X times!" + workshop reminder |
| D+14 email | Last-chance discount expiry warning |

### 5.4 Funnel Value Calculation

| Item | Value |
|------|-------|
| Full workshop price | ₩200,000/team |
| Discount for park converts | ₩170,000/team (₩30,000 off) |
| Converted families/year (per park) | 57 |

The funnel conversion revenue is modest (16% of total). The primary value is (1) direct session revenue and (2) brand exposure, with funnel conversion as a bonus.

## 9. Dong-A Ilbo Value Case

> **Internal Section — Not for Park Distribution.** This section addresses why Dong-A Ilbo should participate in C07 alongside C01, and defines the conditions under which co-branding may be suspended.

### 9.1 Non-Cannibalism: C07 Reaches a Completely Different Audience

C01 (Dong-A Media Center direct workshops) serves **pre-committed, high-intent families** who actively searched for an AI education program, registered in advance, paid ₩200,000, and travel to a fixed downtown venue. This is a demand-capture play: we serve families already in the market.

C07 (Theme Park Popup) serves **passive, non-searching families** who are at the park for entertainment and encounter the program by walking past the education zone. They never typed "AI education" into a search engine. Many would never attend a C01 session.

| Dimension | C01 (Direct Workshop) | C07 (Theme Park Popup) |
|-----------|----------------------|------------------------|
| Audience intent | High (actively seeking AI education) | Zero (came for rides) |
| Session price | ₩200,000/team | ₩50,000/team |
| Venue | Dong-A Media Center, Seoul | Theme park across Korea |
| Annual reach | ~500 families/year (20 sessions × 25 teams) | 1,000,000+ park visitors/year |
| C01 overlap (same families) | 100% | Estimated <3% |

**Conclusion:** C07 does not cannibalize C01. It creates a new customer population that has no alternative path to a Dong-A AI education product.

### 9.2 Dong-A's Passive Income: ₩12.5M/Year with Zero Operational Burden

Under the recommended 30/70 revenue share model, Dong-A Ilbo's 25% of operating profit yields:

| Item | Value |
|------|-------|
| Annual operating profit per park (30/70, Year 2) | ₩50,105,000 |
| Dong-A Ilbo's share (25% of operating profit) | **₩12,526,000/year** |
| Dong-A Ilbo's operational contribution | Brand name + press coverage capability |
| Dong-A Ilbo's capital contribution | Zero |
| Dong-A Ilbo's staffing contribution | Zero |

Filamentree provides all staff, equipment, curriculum, and daily operations. Dong-A Ilbo's contribution is brand association and PR support — both activities they already perform through existing channels at zero marginal cost. The ₩12.5M/year is essentially a **royalty income** for brand licensing in an education context.

### 9.3 Co-Branded Brand Impressions: ₩78.8M/Year in Earned Media Value

Per Section 6.3 (Brand Impression Calculation, Everland), the annual brand exposure value of the co-branded Dong-A Ilbo placement across the education zone is estimated at **₩78,816,000/year** — dominated by ₩75M in SNS earned media value (1,500 parent-sharing events × ₩50,000/post). Every parent who posts "We made a game at [Park] with Dong-A AI Academy #동아AI아카데미" is creating authentic earned media that a newspaper advertisement cannot buy.

This expands Dong-A Ilbo's AI education brand beyond the core metropolitan readership into the families who visit theme parks across Korea — a demographic that skews younger and may not be active Dong-A subscribers yet.

### 9.4 First-Mover AI Education in Mass Entertainment Venues

Chosun Ilbo's theme park education programs (에듀투어, Everland school group content) are focused on journalism experience and career exploration — traditional media company activities. **None involve AI education.** Chosun Ilbo's Samsung-connection to Everland gives them a structural relationship advantage, but they have not moved on AI content.

The window for Dong-A Ilbo to position as **"the first national media brand to bring AI education to theme parks"** exists today. Once Chosun Ilbo moves — or once a tech company (Kakao, Naver, or an education platform) enters the same space — the positioning is gone forever.

If C07 launches in Summer 2027:
- Dong-A earns 1.5–2 years of "AI education at theme parks" brand leadership before any competitor can replicate (theme park procurement cycles: 6-12 months minimum).
- The narrative "Dong-A Ilbo brought AI to where families actually go" is a press story that writes itself.

### 9.5 Brand Risk Ceiling: Dong-A's Right to Suspend Co-Branding

Dong-A Ilbo's brand is at risk if operational failures occur in a high-visibility public environment. The following thresholds trigger Dong-A's right to demand suspension of co-branding (not termination of the program — Filamentree may continue without Dong-A branding):

| Trigger | Threshold | Consequence |
|---------|-----------|------------|
| **Safety incident** | Any participant injury requiring medical attention on-site | Immediate suspension pending investigation; Dong-A brand removed from zone within 48 hours |
| **Participant satisfaction collapse** | Average session score falls below 3.5/5.0 for 4 consecutive weeks | Dong-A co-branding suspended; 30-day remediation window for Filamentree |
| **Media negative coverage** | National press or major portal coverage criticizing the program by name | Suspension within 24 hours of publication; joint crisis response team activated |
